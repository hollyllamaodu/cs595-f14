					<div class="body_inner">



						
<p dir="ltr">Having <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/19/iphone-6-review/">reviewed the iPhone 6</a> and found iOS 8 to be a fast, fluid and error free experience it now seems many owners of older iPhones and iPads have not been so lucky.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Bloggers <a href="http://tech.firstpost.com/news-analysis/apple-ios-8-problems-plague-users-shorter-battery-life-slower-wi-fi-speeds-and-more-235133.html">Tech 2</a> spotted this fast growing&nbsp;<a href="https://discussions.apple.com/thread/6546549">Apple Support Communities thread</a> citing ‘iOS 8 WiFi Problems’ with comments from iPhone 5S, iPad Air and iPad mini owners. The thread has also expanded to complaints about iOS 8 battery life problems, the performance of apps and even the loss of cell phone data.</p> 
<div id="attachment_5994" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 1053px">
 <img class="size-full wp-image-5994" alt="Image courtesy of Apple" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/gordonkelly/files/2014/10/2014-09-22_16-40-201.png" />
 <p class="wp-caption-text">Image courtesy of Apple</p>
</div> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Notably user ‘Morac’ states:</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">“Chatted with <a href="/companies/apple/">Apple</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="AAPL" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/280" data-quotes-closing="99.9" data-quotes-now="99.62"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/apple/">Apple</a></span></span> support and they had me do a restore and set up as new. I did that since I’m seeing other glitches in iOS 8. It seemed to work after that, but I only tested for a few minutes and didn’t do the ping test (which I should have done as that would have given me a definitive answer). I then restored my backup and the issues returned. Apple support implied if restoring as new didn’t fix the problem, then it’s a hardware problem. Considering the number of reports about this, he can’t be correct.”</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">So far Apple has not issued any formal response and no Apple staff have replied to the Support Communities thread which was started on September 20.</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/08/iphone-6-why-apple-has-super-sized-the-iphone/">Read more:&nbsp;Why Apple Made Massive New iPhones</a></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<div id="attachment_5892" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 1950px">
 <img class="size-large wp-image-5892" alt="iOS 8 on the iPhone 6" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/gordonkelly/files/2014/10/ios-8-1940x11271.png" />
 <p class="wp-caption-text">iOS 8 on the iPhone 6</p>
</div> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Comment</strong></p> 
<p dir="ltr">Reports of this nature are not unusual with iOS updates. Apple’s control over hardware and software is typically cited as a major advantage for its fast and reliable iOS roll-outs, but there are still millions of variables such as app combinations and settings which can play havoc.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">As it stands I would play down Tech 2’s description that the software update “plagues users”. iOS 8 updates will run into the 100s of millions and one Communities Support thread – despite appearing popular – is not confirmation of a much wider problem.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">That said these aren’t the first problems we’ve seen with iOS 8. Notably iPhone 4S owners have <a href="http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/upgrading-iphone-4s-ios-8-performance-issues-everything-you-need-know-1466045">complained</a> about greatly reduced performance and on a scale which suggests Apple will need to release a software patch to address it in the near future.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">It is also widely reported that iOS 8 adoption has been <a href="http://www.cnet.com/news/ios-8-adoption-lags-behind-ios-7/">slower than that of iOS 7</a>&nbsp;(possibly due to iPhone 4S owners holding fire), but we will get a more accurate picture after greater time has passed.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">If you have had problems with your iOS 8 update – either along the same lines as the Support Community thread or otherwise – let us know.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">And for those who have upgraded to iOS 8 without a problem you can read about Amadou’s exploration of its <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/amadoudiallo/2014/09/18/apple-ios-8-top-new-features/">best new features</a>, Mark’s take on <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/markrogowsky/2014/09/18/8-misses-in-ios-8/">where it missed the mark</a> and my guide to the <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/22/ios-8-the-best-apple-keyboard-replacements/">best third party keyboards</a>.</p> 
<div></div> 
<div>
 <em>Update: Apple did not respond to requests to comment, but has released iOS 8.0.2. Unfortunately that now appears to be <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/10/02/ios-8-bluetooth-problems/">suffering from Bluetooth connectivity problems</a>.</em>
</div>

    	<div class="comment_content">
    		
<p>On an Apple Forum thread – Ipad mini with retina wifi extremely slow/dead after ios 8 update – several people discuss this same problem. They have come to discover that many of them had dual band routers and that their apple device, after the ios 8 update, wifi became extremely slow if it worked at all even though it showed it was connected. They found that the solution was to switch from 5 Ghz on the router to 2.4 Ghz – after doing so, the devices worked just fine, including my iPad Air after I switched to 2.4 Ghz. The trouble is, I want to connect at 5 Ghz. My other Apple devices (iPad 2 and iPhone 5) that I have not yet updated have been and still are doing just fine at 5 Ghz. Obviously, then, this is a bug in IOS 8 and not a hardware problem. I can only hope that Apple will realize and accept this FACT and fix the bug.</p>
