	<h3 class="entry-title">
			Why Another MVC? 
	    </h3>

    <h4 class="entry-meta">By Yolande Poirier on <a href="#">Sep 10, 2014</a></h4>

    <div class="entry-body">
                                        	<p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Helvetica;">In this <a href="http://www.oracle.com/technetwork/articles/java/mvc-2280472.html">new article</a>, JavaServer Faces (JSF) spec lead Ed Burns explains two kinds of model view controller (MVC) as part of the JSF framework. The article's goal is to show the <span style="font-family: Arial;">continued commitment to JSF evolution and to clarify the complementary usages of these two view technologies. </span></p> 
  <p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Arial; min-height: 14px;"><br /></p> 
  <p style="margin: 0px; font-size: 12px; font-family: Arial;">The two kinds of MVC in question are UI component oriented MVC and action oriented MVC. <a href="http://www.oracle.com/technetwork/articles/java/mvc-2280472.html">This article </a>first goes over these two different styles of MVC, then explains the rationale for filing a separate JSR for MVC 1.0 and explains how this new specification will relate to the next version of JSF.&nbsp;</p> 
  <div> 
    <p><img src="https://blogs.oracle.com/java/resource/UI_Component_Oriented_MVC.png" /><img src="https://blogs.oracle.com/java/resource/ActionOrientedMVC.png" /></p> 
    <p> </p> 
    <p><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 12px;">If you attend JavaOne, you find many <a href="https://oracleus.activeevents.com/2014/connect/search.ww?eventRef=javaone#loadSearch-event=null&amp;searchPhrase=JSF+&amp;searchType=session&amp;tc=0&amp;sortBy=&amp;p=&amp;i(10009)=10111">JavaOne sessions about JSF</a></span></p> 
    <p> </p>
