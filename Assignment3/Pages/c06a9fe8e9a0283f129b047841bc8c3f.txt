		<h1 itemprop="headline">Five Websites Hollywood Can Still Buy Now That Maker And Fullscreen Are Off The Table</h1>



			<!-- Make individual group author appear -->

	<div class="comment_links">
		<a href="#comment_reply" class="scroll_to post_your_comment">Comment Now</a>
		<div class="comments_follow followable_block">




    <div class="followable_block initializing" data-target-type="article" data-target-id="blogAndPostId/blog/post/928-18495" data-object-json="{&quot;title&quot;:&quot;Five Websites Hollywood Can Still Buy Now That Maker And Fullscreen Are Off The Table&quot;,&quot;avatar_src&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;follow_count&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" data-type-advoice="false" data-target-name="" data-tracking-link-name="articleLink_ArticleCommentsFollow">

    <div class="follow_unireg">

	<span class="follow_count"></span>
	<span class="loadingstate"><img class="loadingstateimg" src="http://i.forbesimg.com/assets/img/loading_spinners/16px_on_transparent.gif" alt="" width="16" height="16" /></span>

			<div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">

				<div class="body">

					<div class="body_inner">

							<figure class="featured_image">
								<img src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/dorothypomerantz/files/2014/09/Fullscreen-e1411517313429-1940x1092.jpg" alt="" itemprop="image" />
								<figcaption>Fullscreen's 2014 InTour featured Jc Caylen and Dylan Dauzat</figcaption>
							</figure>


						
<p>Chances are you’ve never heard of Fullscreen and if you know Maker, it’s only because <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/dorothypomerantz/2014/04/01/with-disney-buying-maker-do-all-big-media-companies-need-to-up-their-youtube-game/">Disney recently bought the company</a> for $500 million (that price could go up to $950 million if the company meets certain targets). Both companies are what are known as multi channel networks, online groups of YouTube sites that bring a bunch of creators under one roof. Maker has big stars like <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/PewDiePie">PewDiePie </a>and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/ERB">Epic Rap Battles.&nbsp;</a>&nbsp;Fullscreen has The <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/TheFineBros">Fine Brother</a>s and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/ShaneDawsonTV">Shane Dawson</a>.</p> 
<p>Earlier this week <a href="/companies/att/">AT&amp;T</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="T" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/354" data-quotes-closing="34.97" data-quotes-now="35.36"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/att/">AT&amp;T</a></span></span> and Peter Chernin joined forces to buy Fullscreen in a deal worth a reported $200 to $300 million. Big media companies are buying up these companies to get an immediate, powerful toehold in the digital world. Fullscreen’s network of YouTubers boasts 475 million subscribers and almost 4 billion views in the past month. Chernin, the producer behind films like <em>Planet of the Apes</em> and TV shows like <em>New Girl</em>, can tap the talent on Fullscreen for new shows and he can use the online outlet to help promote his content.</p> 
<p>AT&amp;T can use Fullscreen to help promote its U-Verse high speed Internet service and maybe even offer Fullscreen content as a standalone subscription service.</p> 
<p>“Fullscreen could play into a bundled offering from AT&amp;T,” says Peter Csathy CEO of Manatt Digital Media. “They could sell access to premium channels on Fullscreen.”</p> 
<p>We have yet to see how big media companies actually use these MCNs. It’s tricky because these companies are built on the strength of fans who have (or feel they have) direct relationships with the YouTube stars they follow.</p> 
<p>Those fans don’t want a corporation like Disney or AT&amp;T getting in the way, charging for content or turning their beloved stars into corporate shills.</p> 
<p>But there’s no denying short-form online videos are a big part of the new mix of media which is why you see these companies spending so much on these MCNs.</p> 
<p>Maker and Fullscreen were the two biggest independent MCNs after <a href="http://www.awesomenesstvnetwork.com/">Awesomeness TV</a> was bought by <a href="/companies/dreamworks-animation/">DreamWorks Animation</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="DWA" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/1353" data-quotes-closing="24.83" data-quotes-now="24.71"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/dreamworks-animation/">DreamWorks Animation</a></span></span> last year for $33 million (which could grow to $117 million depending on Awesomeness hitting certain milestones). All of these companies have a broad array of different kinds of channels.</p> 
<p>The MCNs that are left standing are smaller niche sites. Csathy says we can expect Hollywood to turn its attention to this space next when media companies go looking for Internet content. Some sites he believes Hollywood is now eyeing:</p> 
<p><a href="http://stylehaul.com/"><strong>StyleHaul</strong></a></p> 
<p>The fashion and beauty MCN boasts 175 million network subscribers and 4,500 channels like <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/cutepolish">Cute Polish </a>which features tutorials on different kinds of manicures. Bertelsmann’s Digital Media Arm <a href="http://allthingsd.com/20130514/youtubes-stylehaul-network-picks-up-another-6-million-this-time-from-rtl/">has already contributed</a> to two funding rounds totaling $17 million so the German company has the inside track if it wants to buy StyleHaul. But Csathy says he could see a make up or beauty brand buying the site. Look at <a href="/companies/red-bull/">Red Bull</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="null" data-exchange="null" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/101908" data-quotes-closing="0.0" data-quotes-now="0.0"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/red-bull/">Red Bull</a></span></span>. It used be known just as a drink but now it represents a certain kind of lifestyle content online. StyleHaul, which includes original shows like Lisa Kudrow’s <em>Web Therapy</em>, could do the same for the right beauty brand.</p> 
<p><strong><a href="https://www.machinima.com/">Machinima</a></strong></p> 
<p>What StyleHaul is to beauty and fashion, Machinima is to video gaming. Its 175 million monthly hits come mostly from the coveted 18-to-34 male demographic. The company includes video-game playing stars like <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/SkyDoesMinecraft">SkyDoesMinecraft</a> and &nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/JeromeASF?feature=watch">Jerome ASF</a>. In March Warner Bros.<a href="http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/warner-bros-leads-18-million-687244"> led an $18 million </a>investing round in the company after it lost its CEO and laid off 42 people. Chad Gutstein moved from the Ovation network to run Machinima. Expect <a href="/companies/time-warner/">Time Warner</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="TWX" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/4333" data-quotes-closing="73.2" data-quotes-now="74.72"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/time-warner/">Time Warner</a></span></span> to be at the front of the line if the company is sold.</p> 
<p><strong><a href="http://whistlesports.com/">Whistle Sports</a></strong></p> 
<p>Think of it as <a href="/companies/espn/">ESPN</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="null" data-exchange="null" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/91205" data-quotes-closing="0.0" data-quotes-now="0.0"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/espn/">ESPN</a></span></span> for the next generation. The sports content comes from fans and athletes and features tutorials on golf swings alongside GoPro videos of a game called Spikball. With 7.6 million subscribers and 170 channels, its smaller than some other sites but sports sells. If would be a natural fit for an ESPN, a sports league or a company like Gatorade that wants its brand associated with cool, young athletes.</p> 
<p><a href="https://www.tastemade.com/"><strong>Tastemade</strong></a><br /> 
 <!--nextpage--><br /> The MCN for foodies, Tastemade is more focused on branded entertainment than selling ads. The two-year old company, which has 15 million subscribers, has a fall lineup of brand-sponsored shows in partnership with companies like General Mills and Hyundai. <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/cmo/2014/09/19/tastemades-fall-lineup-attracts-big-advertisers-without-ad-sales/">According to <em>The Wall Street Journal</em></a>, &nbsp;General Mills is using Tastemade’s talent to promote the 30th anniversary of Cinnamon Toast Crunch. Hyundai’s show will feature tailgating. Not surprisingly, Scripps Networks (the company behind The Food Network) <a href="http://www.scrippsnetworksinteractive.com/newsroom/company-news/Pages/Tastemade-Closes-%2425-Million-Series-C-Round-of-Funding.aspx">recently led</a> a $25 million investment round in Tastemade.</p> 
<p><a href="http://mituinc.com/home/"><strong>&nbsp;MiTu</strong></a></p> 
<p>With divisions for lifestyles, music and “macho” MiTu is an MCN aimed at Latinos. MiTu boasts 39 million subscribers and 158,000 videos. Chernin, who just bought Fullscreen, owns a piece of MiTu as does Upfront Ventures which was an early investor in Maker. Any company looking to expand into the lucrative Latino market could make a run for MiTu or it could go to an established Hispanic player like Univision or Telemundo.</p>
