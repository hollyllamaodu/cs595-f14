		<h1 itemprop="headline">Apple Leads Q2 Buyback Pack As Share Repurchases Decline Overall</h1>

<p>Is there a stock list that <a href="/companies/apple/">Apple</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="AAPL" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/280" data-quotes-closing="99.9" data-quotes-now="99.62"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/apple/">Apple</a></span></span> doesn’t top? The iPhone maker is the <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/samanthasharf/2014/08/25/sp-crosses-2000-for-first-time-ever/">largest S&amp;P component</a>. Retail investors trade it&nbsp;<a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/samanthasharf/2014/01/10/apple-led-the-pack-of-stocks-retail-investors-traded-most-in-2013/">more than any other stock</a>. The company pays <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/samanthasharf/2013/10/07/apple-buyback-lets-exxon-reclaim-crown-of-biggest-dividend-payer/">more cash in dividends</a>&nbsp;than any other.&nbsp;&nbsp;And today,&nbsp;S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices again confirmed Apple also buys back more of its own shares.</p> 
<p>Apple repurchased $5 billion worth of stock in the second quarter of 2014. According to an analysis released by S&amp;P Tuesday,&nbsp;<a href="/companies/ibm/">IBM</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="IBM" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/2217" data-quotes-closing="186.91" data-quotes-now="188.67"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/ibm/">IBM</a></span></span>, <a href="/companies/exxon-mobil/">Exxon Mobil</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="XOM" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/1530" data-quotes-closing="93.3" data-quotes-now="93.92"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/exxon-mobil/">Exxon Mobil</a></span></span>, <a href="/companies/wells-fargo/">Wells Fargo</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="WFC" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/4683" data-quotes-closing="51.4" data-quotes-now="52.1"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/wells-fargo/">Wells Fargo</a></span></span> and <a href="/companies/intel/">Intel</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="INTC" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/2207" data-quotes-closing="33.51" data-quotes-now="34.03"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/intel/">Intel</a></span></span> also bought big in the second quarter, but overall share repurchases were down 1.6% from the same period last year to $116.2 billion.&nbsp;Apple&nbsp;was not immune from this pattern. Its $5 billion is down sharply from $16 billion in the second quarter of 2013 (a record for any company at the time).</p> 
<div class="forbes_embedded_gallery">
 <p><a class="gallery_embedded_launcher" href="http://www.forbes.com/pictures/ffgh45egefm/the-20-largest-q2-2014-buybacks/">The 20 Largest Q2 2014 Buybacks</a></p>
</div> 
<p>Nevertheless, the overall annual trend is positive. For the 12 months that ended in June, S&amp;P 500 components increased their buyback expenditures 26.6% to $533 billion. The gains were driven in part by a strong first quarter. The $159.3 billion spent on stock buybacks in Q1 made it the second largest repurchase quarter on record.</p> 
<p>Again, Apple ushered the trend.&nbsp;Tim Cook and Co. bought back $18 billion in Q1 (setting a new record). The company’s total so far this calendar year is therefore close to $23 billion. It is therefore likely Apple will beat the $26 billion it bought back in 2013.</p> 
<p>As FORBES Markets&nbsp;Editor <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/steveschaefer/2014/01/23/icahn-lashes-out-at-apple-board-stake-climbs-to-3-6-billion/">Steve Schaefer reported</a>, the CEO was spurred on by billionaire <a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/carl-icahn/">Carl Icahn.</a>&nbsp;The activist investor went on a “crusade” earlier this year&nbsp;insisting Apple buyback more stock to correct what Icahn called “‘a dramatic valuation disconnect’ between the company’s shares and the broader market.” In a February letter to Apple shareholders Icahn called the company “perhaps the most overcapitalized company in corporate history.” Icahn <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/steveschaefer/2014/02/10/icahn-bows-out-of-apple-fight-for-now-credits-tim-cook/">eventually backed down</a>&nbsp;and a few months later Apple announced plans <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/steveschaefer/2014/04/23/apple-splits-stock-7-for-1-will-splash-130b-in-cash-on-investors/">to return $130 billion to shareholders</a> including up to $90 billion in buybacks.</p>
