	<h3 class="entry-title">
			Rich Client and IoT with James Weaver 
	    </h3>

    <h4 class="entry-meta">By Yolande Poirier on <a href="#">Sep 17, 2014</a></h4>

    <div class="entry-body">
                                        	<div><img src="https://blogs.oracle.com/javaone/resource/JimWeaver.png" align="right" />James Weaver is a Java developer, author, and speaker with a passion for Java, rich-client applications, and the Internet of Things (IoT). Weaver has books including <em>Inside Java</em>, <em>Beginning J2EE 1.4</em>, and the <em>Pro JavaFX</em> series. As an Oracle Java Technology Ambassador, Weaver speaks internationally at software technology conferences about Java, JavaFX, and IoT. Weaver tweets as @JavaFXpert, and blogs at http://JavaFXpert.com.&nbsp;</div> 
  <div> 
    <p><strong>Q: What are you looking forward to this year at JavaOne?<span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></strong> </p> 
    <p><strong>Weaver:</strong> Near and dear to my heart is the <a href="https://www.oracle.com/javaone/keynotes/index.html">Community keynote</a> that I am leading this year. It has just been amazing to talk to community members and learn about their projects using Java, Java 8, and IoT. During the Community keynote, we will showcase those amazing people and projects from the community.</p> 
  </div> 
  <p><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"></span>Also, I’m presenting three sessions with different community members. The first one is a “<a href="https://oracleus.activeevents.com/2014/connect/sessionDetail.ww?SESSION_ID=1993">JavaFX 3D</a>” session with Sean Phillips, Zoran Sevarac, and José Pereda. We’ll give examples of JavaFX 3D applications and present the new features of JavaFX 8.&nbsp;</p> 
  <div><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span>The second one is the “<a href="https://oracleus.activeevents.com/2014/connect/sessionDetail.ww?SESSION_ID=3473">JavaFX Community and Ecosystem</a>” session. Alex Casall, Hendrik Ebbers, and I will lead this panel on the state of the JavaFX community and how to get involved. I will highlight books, articles, applications, and other resources that are at the forefront of JavaFX development.&nbsp;</div> 
  <div><br /></div> 
  <div><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span>My third session is &quot;<a href="https://oracleus.activeevents.com/2014/connect/sessionDetail.ww?SESSION_ID=1863">Creating Our Robot Overlords: Autonomous Drone Development with Java and the Internet of Things</a>&quot; with Mark Heckler and Sean Phillips about autonomous drones. We will use the quadcopter, an ARdrone, and demonstrate how we can hook a Raspberry Pi, which is a very small embedded device, to the drone to make it fly autonomously.&nbsp;</div> 
  <div><br /></div> 
  <div><strong>Q: What are some of the highlights of the <a href="https://www.oracle.com/javaone/tracks/index.html#internet">IoT track</a>?&nbsp;</strong> </div> 
  <div> 
    <p><strong>Weaver: </strong>IoT is fairly new and represents a broad area with many facets. The Strategy and Technical keynotes as well as the Community keynote will present different aspects of IoT. We’ll also dive into a lot of those technologies. For example, James Gosling, the founder of Java, is working on the Wave Glider at &nbsp;Liquid Robotics. The Wave Glider is a robot that collects and transmits ocean data.&nbsp;</p> 
  </div> 
  <div>There will be sessions on standards, protocols like Message Queue Telemetry Transport (MQTT), &nbsp;different domains, applications in healthcare, retail home automation, and security. With processing power getting smaller and devices being distributed onto several systems, security becomes challenging. Java is very uniquely positioned to respond to security concerns because security isn’t an afterthought with Java. It is built into the platform. I think attendees are going to learn a lot about IoT, with types of applications, challenges, and examples.&nbsp;</div> 
  <div><br /></div> 
  <div><strong>Q: How do you suggest web developers get started with IoT?&nbsp;</strong></div> 
  <div> 
    <p><strong>Weaver:</strong> I recommend they attend sessions about front-end technologies such as JavaFX as well as HTML5 technologies using Java—especially, the sessions about front-end technologies on small devices. They will get ideas on how to develop and deploy applications across devices. In the Community keynote, we will present applications using Java EE on the back-end and different devices on the front-end</p> 
  </div> 
  <div><strong>Q: Aside from your sessions, what do you have planned for JavaOne?</strong> </div> 
  <div> 
    <p><strong>Weaver:</strong> I’ll also have a lot of fun at JavaOne. I’ll be playing in a Java band with Java developers—for example, Freddy Guime, Frank Greco, Geert Bevin, Mattias Karlsson, and Zoran Sevarac. We’re going to be entertaining anybody who can stand our music. We’ll play a couple of times at Duke's Cafe,&nbsp;Taylor Street as well as at the JCP event&nbsp;</p> 
  </div> 
  <div><strong>Q: Are you playing real instruments, or are there digital instruments powered by a Raspberry Pi?</strong></div> 
  <div> 
    <p><strong>Weaver:</strong> We’ll be playing real instruments such as guitars and drums. Mattias will probably play drum pads. Geert Bevin will play the guitar and probably the Eigenharp, a cutting-edge digital instrument. It’s going to be fun!&nbsp;</p> 
  </div> 
  <div><br /></div>
