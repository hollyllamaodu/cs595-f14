		<h1 itemprop="headline">Apple's iOS 8 Rapidly Overtakes Android's KitKat In Under Five Days</h1>



			<!-- Make individual group author appear -->

				<div class="body">

					<div class="body_inner">

							<figure class="featured_image">
								<img src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/ewanspence/files/2014/09/iphone6plus_feature.jpg" alt="The iPhone 6 Plus (image: Ewan Spence)" itemprop="image" />
								<figcaption>The iPhone 6 Plus (image: Ewan Spence)</figcaption>
							</figure>


						
<p>Alongside the release of <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/chuckjones/2014/09/22/tim-cook-does-it-again-with-over-10-million-iphone-6-sales/">ten million</a>&nbsp;<a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/19/iphone-6-review/">iPhone 6</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/ewanspence/2014/09/19/apple-iphone-6-plus-review-size-really-does-matter/">iPhone 6 Plus</a>&nbsp;handsets, Apple also released the next version of its&nbsp;mobile&nbsp;operating system for its&nbsp;older devices.<a href="https://www.apple.com/iphone-6/ios-8/"> iOS 8 is a major release of the platform</a>, and five days after its launch, one in four iOS devices are&nbsp;running the latest version of the OS.</p> 
<p>Five days after the public release of iOS 8, it has been installed on 25.95% of eligible iPads and iPhones. That compares to an installation rate of&nbsp;48.41% for iOS 7, and 41.07% of iOS 6, in the same timescale. The&nbsp;<a href="http://www.fiksu.com/resources/ios_8_tracker">data was collated by&nbsp;Fiksu</a>&nbsp;from&nbsp;its&nbsp;cross-platform mobile&nbsp;marketing platform.</p> 
<div id="attachment_12464" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 970px">
 <img class="size-full wp-image-12464" alt="iOS 8 Usage Data (compiled by Fiksu)" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/ewanspence/files/2014/09/ios8_usage_fiksu.jpg" />
 <p class="wp-caption-text">iOS 8 Usage Data (compiled by Fiksu)</p>
</div> 
<p>While the lower percentage figures may feel like a failure, the increased user base of Apple devices will have contributed to that lower percentage. Apple’s user base will&nbsp;consist of more regular users, who <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/markrogowsky/2014/09/17/ios-8-arrives-should-you-upgrade-right-now/">will not be ready to jump onto a new OS release the second they are alerted to it</a>. It’s probably more important to look at the shape of the adoption curve. In the case of iOS 6, iOS 7, and iOS 8, the peaks, curves, and troughs, are all following a&nbsp;similar pattern, albeit with more smoothing on the iOS 8 curve.</p> 
<p>The trend for quickly&nbsp;updating the OS continues, and Apple should be proud of the uptake in such a short period.</p> 
<p>The rapid adoption of iOS 8 will be a boon for developers. With a limited portfolio of devices and operating systems to consider, Apple suffers from less fragmentation than Android,&nbsp;but developers will be itching to focus on the new features and tools available in the iOS 8 SDK. The faster the user-base moves to the new system, the smoother an experience developers will have.</p> 
<p>The&nbsp;percentage of iOS users on the latest OS (in five days) is&nbsp;ahead of the installed base of Android 4.4 KitKat (<a href="http://developer.android.com/about/versions/kitkat.html">launched on October 31&nbsp;2013</a>). According to&nbsp;<a href="https://developer.android.com/about/dashboards/index.html">Google’s own figures</a>, Android 4.4 ‘KitKat’ has a current distribution base of 24.5% after ten months and twenty-two days.</p> 
<p>It took Apple less than five days to overhaul those numbers.</p>
