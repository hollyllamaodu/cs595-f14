					<h1 class="block-entry-title"><a href="http://ideas.ted.com/2014/09/18/need-to-know-scotland-unicorns-and-the-bearded-troll-that-is-great-britain/" rel="bookmark">Need to know: Scotland, unicorns and “the bearded troll that is Great&nbsp;Britain”</a></h1>

												<a href="http://ideas.ted.com/author/lauramcclurehoughton/" title="Posts by Laura McClure" class="url fn" rel="author">Laura McClure</a>

<h2>“Should Scotland be an independent country?” is a question Scots answered &#8220;no&#8221; to this week. Why did Scotland consider breaking up with the UK? Who else has stopped being British over the past few centuries? And what&#8217;s the deal with Scottish unicorns, anyway? 3 ideas behind the news.</h2>
<p><a title="Guardian on Scotland for non-Brits" href="http://www.theguardian.com/politics/video/2014/sep/17/scottish-referendum-explained-for-non-brits-video" target="_blank"><strong>Source: “Scottish referendum explained for non-Brits,”</strong></a> the Guardian, 2014.<br />
<strong>Why you should watch this animation:</strong> If your world geography knowledge ends with &#8220;Italy is shaped like a boot,&#8221; this animated primer is for you. From how to find Scotland on a map &#8212; “Scotland is the crazy hat worn by the bearded troll that is Great Britain” &#8212; to Scotland&#8217;s torrid past with England, this cheeky animation will get you up to speed on the history behind <a title="Are the scots independent yet?" href="http://www.arethescotsindependentyet.com" target="_blank">Scotland&#8217;s vote to become an independent country</a>.</p>
<p><strong><a title="map of countries that left great britain" href="http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/news/regions/europe/united-kingdom/140915/map-formerly-united-kingdom-independence-from-britain" target="_blank">Source: “This map shows all the countries that have declared independence from the British,”</a></strong> Global Post, 2014.<br />
<strong>Why you should marvel at this map:</strong> Scotland&#8217;s just the latest country to want to jilt the British. This map illustrates an important point: Lots of countries have seceded from British control over the years. Check out this map to learn which nations declared independence and when they left <a title="union jack new yorker" href="http://www.newyorker.com/news/news-desk/referendum-on-union-jack" target="_blank">the Union Jack</a>.</p>
<p><strong><a title="Scotland unicorns money" href="http://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-27294419" target="_blank">Source: “Scottish independence: How many unicorns do you earn?”</a></strong> BBC, 2014.<br />
<strong>Why you should read this article:</strong> As <a title="John Oliver on Scotland" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-YkLPxQp_y0" target="_blank">John Oliver joyfully pointed out</a>, Scotland&#8217;s national beast is way cooler than America&#8217;s bald eagle. But did you know that in 1503, the Scottish unicorn was also a coin worth about 20 shillings? &#8220;You could also carry about a half-unicorn, which wasn&#8217;t as horrific as it sounds,&#8221; notes the BBC. For more about Scottish currency, read this article.</p>
<p><a title="Alex Salmond TED Global 2012" href="http://blog.ted.com/2012/06/27/how-small-countries-make-a-big-impact-alex-salmond-at-tedglobal-2012/" target="_blank"><strong>+ Read about Alex Salmond&#8217;s remarks at TEDGlobal 2012.</strong></a></p>
