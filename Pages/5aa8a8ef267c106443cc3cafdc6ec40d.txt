	<h3 class="entry-title">
			Published: JavaFX Rich Client Programming on the NetBeans Platform
	    </h3>

    <h4 class="entry-meta">By Geertjan on <a href="#">Sep 19, 2014</a></h4>

    <div class="entry-body">
                                        	<p>Gail and Paul Anderson (<a href="http://netbeans.dzone.com/interviews/javafx-rich-client-programming">interviewed a few days ago here</a>) passed on the happy news to me today. 
The eBook of <a href="http://www.amazon.com/JavaFX-Client-Programming-NetBeans-Platform/dp/0321927710">JavaFX Rich Client Programming on the NetBeans Platform</a>&nbsp;is available now. The physical tree based book (i.e., in addition to the eBook, there will be a treeBook!) will be available from the end of the month, when it will be released during JavaOne 2014.&nbsp;If you'll be at JavaOne 2014, please come to the book signing at the JavaOne bookstore, Tuesday, September 30, 15:30 PM - 16:00 PM.</p> 
  <p>The book guides you in a lot of detail, with many samples, to the creation of this application:</p> 
  <p><a href="http://netbeans.dzone.com/sites/all/files/familytreeapp-1.png"><img src="http://netbeans.dzone.com/sites/all/files/familytreeapp-2.png" /></a> </p> 
  <p>Here's the Google link (no formats specified, but devices listed):</p> 
  <p><a href="http://books.google.com/books/about/JavaFX_Rich_Client_Programming_on_the_Ne.html?id=-WSJBAAAQBAJ">http://books.google.com/books/about/JavaFX_Rich_Client_Programming_on_the_Ne.html?id=-WSJBAAAQBAJ

</a></p> 
  <p>Also, on the publisher's site  (formats EPUB, MODI, PDF):</p> 
  <p><a href="http://www.informit.com/store/javafx-rich-client-programming-on-the-netbeans-platform-9780321927712">http://www.informit.com/store/javafx-rich-client-programming-on-the-netbeans-platform-9780321927712

</a></p> 
  <p>And Kindle on Amazon:</p> 
  <p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/JavaFX-Client-Programming-NetBeans-Platform/dp/0321927710/"> http://www.amazon.com/JavaFX-Client-Programming-NetBeans-Platform/dp/0321927710/</a></p> 
  <p>Take note that the links above also provide access to quite a lot of free sample materials, e.g., complete and partial chapters, to give you a taste of the book's more than 900 pages providing clear explanations and heaps of code samples in the context of the Family Tree application.&nbsp;</p> 
  <p>I had the honor of writing the foreword to the book. Here it is, hope it gives a sense of how awesome and useful this book is! </p> 
  <p><img style="border-width: 1px; border-style: solid; border-color: black;" src="https://blogs.oracle.com/geertjan/resource/asgteach-nbp-foreword.png" /></p> 
  <p>Congratulations, Gail and Paul, and to Greg Doench from Prentice Hall for having faith in this project!</p>
        
    </div>
