	<h3 class="entry-title">
			Robots, NetBeans IDE, and Raspberry Pi with James Gosling 
	    </h3>

    <h4 class="entry-meta">By Yolande Poirier on <a href="#">Sep 16, 2014</a></h4>

    <div class="entry-body">
                                        	<div><img src="https://blogs.oracle.com/javaone/resource/JamesGosling.png" align="right" />James Gosling is now chief software architect of the Wave Glider, the flagship product at Liquid Robotics. The Wave Glider is a self-propelled, autonomous marine robot that collects and transmits ocean data. The Wave Glider will be on display exclusively at the <a href="https://www.oracle.com/javaone/javahub/index.html">Java Hub</a> in the JavaOne exhibit hall during the entire conference.&nbsp;</div> 
  <div><br /></div> 
  <div>Since Gosling started at Liquid Robotics, he has re-architected the onboard software and refined a data-as-a-service cloud to provide direct, real-time access to ocean information. Java, which he invented, has played an increasing role in ocean data transmission and analysis. &nbsp;</div> 
  <div> 
    <p>“Being able to debug and profile robots out at sea is a truly life-altering experience,” Gosling explains. He uses a set of tools—consisting of editors, debuggers, and profilers—that are part of the NetBeans IDE. At the <a href="https://netbeans.org/community/articles/javaone/2014/netbeans-day-2014.html">JavaOne 2014 NetBeans Community Day</a>, he will present the session “James Gosling, Robots, the Raspberry Pi, and Small Devices” <a href="https://oracleus.activeevents.com/2014/connect/sessionDetail.ww?SESSION_ID=8907">[UGF8907]</a> on Sunday, September 28. He will also present “Debugging and Profiling Robots with James Gosling” <a href="https://oracleus.activeevents.com/2014/connect/sessionDetail.ww?SESSION_ID=6699">[CON6699]</a> on Wednesday, October 1. Geertjan Wielenga, Mark Heckler, José Pereda, Johannes Weigend, Shai Almog and Jens Deters will join him to discuss those two topics.&nbsp;</p> 
    <p>   
Join him as he closes out the JavaOne Community Keynote with a fun, historical perspective of the genesis of Java, and a T-shirt toss!  The Community Keynote will be held in the Marriott Marquis, Salon 7/8/9, on Thursday, October 2, 2014
</p> 
  </div>
