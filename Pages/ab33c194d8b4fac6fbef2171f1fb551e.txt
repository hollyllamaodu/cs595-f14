		<h1 itemprop="headline">iPhone 'Bendgate' Is Apple's Mea Culpa</h1>



			<!-- Make individual group author appear -->

	<div class="comment_links">
		<a href="#comment_reply" class="scroll_to post_your_comment">Comment Now</a>
		<div class="comments_follow followable_block">




    <div class="followable_block initializing" data-target-type="article" data-target-id="blogAndPostId/blog/post/2907-6054" data-object-json="{&quot;title&quot;:&quot;iPhone 'Bendgate' Is Apple's Mea Culpa&quot;,&quot;avatar_src&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;follow_count&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" data-type-advoice="false" data-target-name="" data-tracking-link-name="articleLink_ArticleCommentsFollow">

			<div class="article_body article" itemprop="articleBody">

				<div class="body">

					<div class="body_inner">



						
<p dir="ltr">I like the iPhone 6, <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/19/iphone-6-review/">a lot</a>. My fellow contributor Ewan likes the iPhone 6 Plus, <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/ewanspence/2014/09/19/apple-iphone-6-plus-review-size-really-does-matter/">a lot</a>. Both models have flaws but they also set new benchmarks in smartphone design which rivals need to match. Or at least they did until <a href="/companies/apple/">Apple</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="AAPL" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/280" data-quotes-closing="99.9" data-quotes-now="99.62"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/apple/">Apple</a></span></span> shot itself in the foot.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">#Bendgate is the predictable term that has flooded social media over the last 24 hours. It comes after a video from <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=znK652H6yQM">Unbox Therapy</a> (below) went viral after demonstrating that it was relatively easy to bend the iPhone 6 Plus. The video itself was a response to scattered reports of the iPhone 6 Plus bending in owners’ front and back pockets.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><iframe class="youtube-player" type="text/html" width="485" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/znK652H6yQM" frameborder="0"></iframe></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">This started a flame war between Apple fans and haters and a number of defences were written including ‘<a href="http://www.wired.com/2014/09/iphone-6-plus-bendgate/">Duh: Of Course the iPhone 6 Plus Can Bend in Your Pocket</a>’ on Wired. Unbox Therapy then poured petrol on these flames with a second video (below) showing not only that trying to straighten a bent iPhone 6 Plus can shatter the screen, but that under the same tests a Galaxy Note 3 was fine.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><iframe class="youtube-player" type="text/html" width="485" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/FwM4ypi3at0" frameborder="0"></iframe></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Suddenly the argument that ‘all phones bend, stupid’ was no longer a great defence. They may, but the iPhone 6 Plus bends <em>more</em>.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Antennagate v2.0</strong></p> 
<p dir="ltr">So where does the blame lie? From my perspective I don’t think it can be shifted from Apple.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">If the iPhone 6 Plus is vulnerable in a pocket it should contain a suitable warning to that effect. Expecting users to change age old habits without warning is impossible and, like ‘<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPhone_4#Antenna">Antennagate</a>’, the whole situation seems to have arrived through a lack of real world testing.</p> 
<div id="attachment_5881" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 1827px">
 <img class="size-full wp-image-5881" alt="New iPhones are ultra thin (image credit: Gordon Kelly)" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/gordonkelly/files/2014/09/IMG_20140919_084820.jpg" />
 <p class="wp-caption-text">New iPhones are ultra thin (image credit: Gordon Kelly)</p>
</div> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Yet more to the point Bendgate also exists because of Apple being Apple. I’ve yet to have a reader tell me the iPhone 5S was too thick (feel free to do so!), but I’ve seen many complain about battery life.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Of course the new iPhones do have improved battery life, but they could’ve revolutionised smartphone and phablet usage had Apple made them just thick enough to fit a flush camera – the lack of which Apple has been keen to <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/16/iphone-6-camera-bulge/">hide in promotional photos</a>. Two problems: one stone.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">But Apple&nbsp;didn’t because more than a decade of unprecedented success has made the company confident it knows what its users want better than they know themselves. In many cases Apple has been proved right, until now. After all pride often comes before a fall.</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/08/iphone-6-why-apple-has-super-sized-the-iphone/">Read more – iPhone 6: Why Apple Made Massive New iPhones</a></p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<div id="attachment_5888" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 1950px">
 <img class="size-large wp-image-5888" alt="iPhone 6 protruding camera (image credit: Gordon Kelly)" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/gordonkelly/files/2014/09/IMG_20140919_083908-1940x1052.jpg" />
 <p class="wp-caption-text">iPhone 6 protruding camera (image credit: Gordon Kelly)</p>
</div> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr"><strong>Wider Industry Fallout</strong></p> 
<p dir="ltr">There is an inevitable (and in many ways understandable) reaction: mock Apple. Go ahead, enjoy it – needlessly skinny phone, bulbous camera, snaps like a twig – but owners of rival handsets should know this could be every bit as damaging for them in the long run as for iPhone owners.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Why? Because like it or not Apple remains the single biggest driving force in the industry. I’m an Android user with no inclination to switch, but I recognise <a href="/companies/google/">Google</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="GOOGL" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/1821" data-quotes-closing="580.885" data-quotes-now="585.93"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/google/">Google</a></span></span> and <a href="/companies/microsoft/">Microsoft</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="MSFT" data-exchange="NASDAQ" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/2854" data-quotes-closing="45.76" data-quotes-now="46.09"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/microsoft/">Microsoft</a></span></span>’s long adoption of NFC will only gain real world traction now Apple is aboard.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">For me Android has surpassed iOS and ‘Android L’ has me drooling over videos on <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q8TXgCzxEnw">Material Design</a>, but there is no disputing the early influence of iOS and had the original iPhone not broken new ground (the first <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTC_Dream">Android phone had a keyboard</a>) I suspect Google’s OS would be a very different and less impressive thing today.</p> 
<p>
 <!--nextpage--></p> 
<div id="attachment_6056" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 1950px">
 <img class="size-large wp-image-6056" alt="HTC One M8 (image credit: Gordon Kelly)" src="http://blogs-images.forbes.com/gordonkelly/files/2014/09/IMG_20140924_175621-1940x949.jpg" />
 <p class="wp-caption-text">HTC One M8 (image credit: Gordon Kelly)</p>
</div> 
<p>&nbsp;</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Apple’s industry influence brings out the best in its rivals – whether playing catch up or inspiring them to leap ahead, but where the majority of Android phones are still behind iPhones (<a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/03/25/htc-one-m8-hands-on-review-first-impressions-suggest-stiff-competition-for-the-samsung-galaxy-s5/">HTC One</a> aside – pictured above) is design. Or they were until the tacky design of the otherwise brilliant Note range was shown to be structurally superior to the latest and greatest iPhones.</p> 
<p dir="ltr">Consequently I’m concerned. I don’t want Apple rivals to feel venerated when they should be using the iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus as the springboard to new and better things. Apple just showed the world metal chassis smartphones don’t need to be heavy, now go prove they don’t need to bend as well!</p> 
<p>It is an exciting time for Apple rivals, if they handle it the right way (that means <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/briancaulfield/2011/11/23/samsung-ad-portrays-apple-customers-as-pack-of-hipster-doofuses/">with dignity Samsung</a>). Combined with <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/gordonkelly/2014/09/22/ios-8-wifi-and-battery-life-problems/">iOS 8 upgrade problems</a>,&nbsp;Apple suddenly has its hands full placating angry iPhone owners, addressing warranty questions and convincing hesitant upgraders to still make the jump.</p> 
<p>The opportunity for rivals to evolve is there if they approach it correctly. The ‘iFanny Pack’ will not be an easy sell.</p> 
<p>&nbsp;</p>
