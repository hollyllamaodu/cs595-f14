Climate Summit in New York City, the White House announced public and private commitments to cut emissions of hydrofluorocarbons or HFCs, factory-made gases used in many cars, homes and buildings for air conditioning or refrigeration.</p><p>The pledges by 19 companies and two industry groups cover the entire HFC supply chain, from the chemical's production to its use in manufacturing and stores. The administration said it will reduce its use in federal buildings, approve more climate-friendly alternatives and fund research to develop next-generation cooling technologies.</p><p>HFCs, which largely replaced ozone-depleting chemicals such as Freon, accounts for a small share of the world's heat-trapping greenhouse gases, but it's nearly 10,000 times as potent as carbon dioxide. <span>Without action, its emissions are expected to nearly double by 2020 and triple by 2030.</span></p><p>"The good progress we are making on restoring the earth's ozone layer would not have been possible without a strong public-private partnership," said Environmental Protection Agency Administrator Gina McCarthy. "By working together again, we can tackle the challenges of climate change."</p><p>The White House announcement came on International Ozone Day, created by the United Nations General Assembly in 1994 to mark the official beginning of the Montreal Protocol, an environment treaty ratified by 196 countries to rid the world of ozone-depleting substances. Last week, a U.N. report said the phase-out of these chemicals is finally allowing the recovery of the ozone layer, which<span id="articleText"> shields life from cancer-causing solar rays.</span></p><p>"Today's actions will build momentum for an amendment to the Montreal Protocol to phase down the global production and consumptions of HFCs," said the White House's <span>Council on Environmental Quality. The CEQ said the new steps could cut greenhouse gases the equivalent of taking nearly 15 million cars off the road for 10 years.</span></p><p><span>The Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute, an industry group, announced it will invest $5 billion over the next decade to research and develop new refrigerants and the equipment in which they'll be used. </span></p><p><span> PepsiCo, the soft-drink maker, announced it will try to buy only HFC-free vending machines and other point-of-sale equipment by 2020 and will begin such U.S. purchases next year.</span></p><p><span> Coca-Cola, which has set a similar goal for its new cold drink equipment, said it has bought 20,000 HFC-free units this year in the USA and now has more than 1 million such units worldwide.</span></p><p><span> Red Bull, maker of energy drinks, said it will order an estimated 32,000 climate-friendly hydrocarbon coolers for 2015.</span></p><p><span> Target, retailer with nearly 1,800 U.S. stores, recently opened two cold-storage facilities that use ammonia, an HFC-free refrigerant, and plans to open two stores next year that use carbon dioxide refrigeration. It already has five stores with the CO2 technology.</span></p><p><span> Kroger, the supermarket chain, said it's joining the EPA's Green Chill program as part of its push to use advanced refrigeration technologies wherever feasible and share its best practices for reducing greenhouse gas emissions.</span></p><p><span> DuPont, the company that invented fluorinated refrigerants, said it has five new products for use in cars and buildings that will reduce emissions and can also boost energy efficiency. It said they can be used in a lot of existing equipment with "minimal" changes.</span></p><p><span>"We see strong global demand for these products," says Kathryn McCord, global business director of DuPont Fluorochemicals. By the end of 2014, she expects 3 million cars in the USA and Europe will use its new Opteon YF refrigerant, which emits 99.9% fewer greenhouse gases than the product it replaces.</span></p><p><span>Other companies signing on to the Obama administration's HFC phasedown include: Arkema, Carrier, Danfoss, Emerson Climate Technologies, Goodman Manufacturing, Hillphoenix, Honeywell, Johnson Controls, Lapolla, Mission Pharmacal, SEVO Systems, Thermo King and True Manufacturing.</span></p><div id="module-position-NjaFdO-RLoI" class="story-asset inline-share-tools-asset"><div class="inline-share-tools asset-inline-share-tools asset-inline-share-tools-bottom"><span
