		<h1 itemprop="headline">Young Physicians OK With Obamacare While Older Doctors Bristle</h1>

				<div class="body">

					<div class="body_inner">



						
<p>Though U.S. physicians continue to have issues with the Affordable Care Act, it appears to be a generational taste developing of the law making sweeping changes to their profession.</p> 
<p>More than half, or 50.8 percent, of doctors 46 years of age and older give the <a href="http://www.forbes.com/health/">health</a> law a “D” or “F” while just 37 percent of doctors 45 and younger give the legislation such poor grades, <a href="http://www.physiciansfoundation.org/">a new survey released </a>last week of physicians commissioned <a title="Physicians Foundation " href="http://www.physiciansfoundation.org/">by The Physicians Foundation.</a></p> 
<p>Meanwhile, nearly 30 percent of the younger doctors gave the health law an “A” or “B” when asked: “What overall grade would you give the Affordable Care Act as a vehicle for healthcare reform?” One-third, or 33.1 percent of doctors 45 and younger gave the law a “C.”</p> 
<p>By comparison, just 22.9 percent of the older doctors gave the law an “A” or “B” with 26 percent giving it a “C,” t<a title="The Physicians Foundation study " href="http://www.physiciansfoundation.org/">he survey shows.</a></p> 
<p>The survey comes as the ACA and other forces are moving doctors away from fee-for-service medicine that allows doctors to order procedures and tests liberally as opposed to more value-based care that encourages evidence-based approaches to treatment and reimbursement for keeping patients healthy.</p> 
<p>A reason for the generational differences could be that younger doctors aren’t as close to the bureaucracy of insurance and changes in reimbursement since more physicians are leaving private practice to become employed.</p> 
<div style="padding: 20px 0pt;margin: 20px 0pt;border-bottom: 1px solid #DDDDDD;border-top: 1px solid #DDDDDD">
 <a style="text-decoration:none;color:#900;font: bold 20px/26px Georgia" href="http://www.forbes.com/ebooks/inside-obamacare-the-fix-for-americas-ailing-health-care-system/?utm_source=articlelink&amp;utm_medium=direct&amp;utm_campaign=obamacareebook">The Forbes eBook On Obamacare</a>
 <br />
 <a style="text-decoration:none;font: 15px/18px  Georgia;color:#000" href="http://www.forbes.com/ebooks/inside-obamacare-the-fix-for-americas-ailing-health-care-system/?utm_source=articlelink&amp;utm_medium=direct&amp;utm_campaign=obamacareebook"><em>Inside Obamacare: The Fix For America’s Ailing Health Care System</em> explores the ways the Affordable Care Act will affect your health care and is available for download now.</a>
</div> 
<p>“Physicians are younger, more are working in employed practice settings and more are leaving private practice,” said Dr. Walker Ray, vice president of The Physicians Foundation and chair of its research committee.</p> 
<p>No matter the age of the doctor, they are feeling the impact of the health law and the more than 8 million Americans who signed up for subsidized private coverage for this year. In addition, millions more Americans are getting coverage via the expanded Medicaid insurance program for the poor.</p> 
<p>These newly insured patients are contributing to a stress on the physician workforce with “81 percent of physicians” describing themselves as “either over-extended or at full capacity,” the foundation said.</p> 
<p>Here are some other highlights of the survey, conducted online of 20,000 physicians from March 2014 through June 2014 for the foundation by physician staffing firm MerrittHawkins.</p> 
<ul> 
 <li>&nbsp; &nbsp; 56 percent of physicians describe their morale as very negative to somewhat negative</li> 
 <li>&nbsp; &nbsp; 17 percent of doctors are in solo practices compared to 25 percent in 2012</li> 
 <li>&nbsp; &nbsp; 39 percent of physicians say they will “accelerate their retirement plans” due to changes in health care</li> 
 <li>&nbsp; &nbsp; 71 percent of doctors say they would still become a physician if they had to do it all over again</li> 
</ul>

The Forbes eBook&nbsp;</i><a href="http://www.forbes.com/ebooks/inside-obamacare-the-fix-for-americas-ailing-health-care-system/">Inside Obamacare</a>: The Fix For America’s Ailing Health Care System<i>answers that question and more. Available now&nbsp;<a href="http://www.amazon.com/Inside-Obamacare-Americas-Ailing-Health-ebook/dp/B00JEPT4PE/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1396449997&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=inside+obamacare" target="_blank">at Amazon</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/book/inside-obamacare-fix-for-americas/id853378821?mt=11" target="_blank">Apple</a>.</i></b></p>
